# Measurement Loop

## Purpose

Use analytics to improve topic choice, format choice, hook quality, and channel mix.
Do not drown in vanity dashboards.

## What to care about first

### Blog
- qualified traffic
- time on page / depth signals if available
- clicks into product or trial paths
- whether the piece becomes strong derivative material

### LinkedIn
- impressions relative to baseline
- engagement quality, not just raw count
- profile or company-page visits
- clicks when a CTA exists
- whether the angle deserves a second variation

### X
- reach
- engagement rate
- link clicks when relevant
- whether the take earned resonance or died fast

### Facebook
- only track enough to know whether selective syndication is worth continuing

## Practical scorecard

After a post or batch, capture:
- topic
- hook type
- format
- channel
- visual or no visual
- CTA type
- result, winner / neutral / loser
- note on why it likely performed that way

## Winner rules

If something works:
- reuse the angle in another format
- make a follow-up piece
- turn the argument into a blog if it started as social
- turn the best section of a blog into a carousel or short thread

## Loser rules

If something flops:
- do not automatically conclude the topic is dead
- first ask whether the failure came from:
  - weak hook
  - wrong channel
  - bad timing
  - unclear proof
  - too much explanation
  - weak visual support

Then either:
- rewrite and retry once
- move the idea to a better channel
- kill it and move on

## Monthly review questions

Ask:
- which pillars are actually producing signal?
- which channels are worth the effort?
- which formats punch above their weight?
- which hooks repeatedly work?
- what deserves a mini-series or recurring slot?
- what should we stop wasting time on?

## Reuse principle

The point of the system is not endless novelty.
The point is to find strong truths and express them repeatedly in better forms.

## Analytics source hierarchy

Prefer this order when possible:
1. business outcome truth
2. channel-native analytics from Postiz or the platform
3. qualitative read on comments or reactions
4. vanity metrics only as supporting color

If a post gets attention but drives no meaningful next step, treat it as interesting, not automatically successful.
