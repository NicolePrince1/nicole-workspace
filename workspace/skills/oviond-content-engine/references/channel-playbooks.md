# Channel Playbooks

## General rule

Same idea, different packaging.
Do not dump identical copy everywhere and pretend it is strategy.

## Blog

### Role
- depth engine
- SEO asset
- source material for repurposing
- proof and education layer

### Best uses
- product walkthroughs
- comparisons
- reporting best practices
- category arguments
- agency operations pieces
- deeper commentary on measurement, onboarding, or retention

### Structure
- strong plain-language headline
- direct intro that states the problem
- 3 to 5 substantive sections
- specific proof, examples, or screenshots where possible
- clear CTA at the end

### Standard
A blog should feel worth reading even if nobody ever sees the derivative social posts.

## LinkedIn

### Role
- primary social channel for Oviond
- thought leadership plus product proof
- strongest lane for agency-owner and operator reach

### Best uses
- one sharp argument
- contrarian but credible take
- feature proof with screenshot or carousel
- short story from support, sales, or operations
- blog distillation with a stronger native hook

### Style
- hook fast
- plain language
- short paragraphs
- one clear takeaway
- moderate polish, not sterile polish

### Good post shapes
- problem -> truth -> implication
- common belief -> why it is wrong -> better view
- screenshot -> what it shows -> why it matters
- lesson list from one real issue

### Avoid
- empty inspiration
- long throat-clearing intros
- fake founder-style vulnerability theatre
- buzzword salad

## X

### Role
- fast ideas
- sharper positioning
- repetition and iteration
- broader category reach

### Best uses
- punchy single-take posts
- short threads
- launches
- product observations
- commentary on reporting, attribution, AI, and agency operations

### Style
- sharper
- more compressed
- more opinionated
- less explanation unless in a thread

### Good post shapes
- one-line contrarian take
- 3 to 7 post thread
- product screenshot with one insight
- short list with a hard opinion

### Avoid
- generic engagement bait
- fake controversy
- long bloated threads with no payoff

## Facebook Page

### Role
- credibility support
- selective syndication
- light community visibility

### Best uses
- major announcements
- product updates
- selected educational posts
- blog shares with cleaner explanatory framing

### Style
- clear and readable
- less edgy than X
- less essay-like than LinkedIn

### Rule
Do not force a Facebook post for every idea. It is a support lane, not the strategic center.

## Instagram

### Role
- optional visual proof lane
- mainly for carousels, short product snippets, branded visuals

### Use only when
- the idea is visually explainable
- design bandwidth exists
- the content will not become generic motivational sludge

## Threads

### Role
- optional spillover from X/LinkedIn ideas
- lightweight experimentation

### Rule
Only use if we can maintain it without diluting the primary channels.

## Cross-channel adaptation matrix

### Blog -> LinkedIn
- pull out one argument, not the whole article
- use a new hook, not the article title pasted raw

### Blog -> X
- compress to one insight or a short thread
- make it feel native and faster

### LinkedIn -> X
- shorten, sharpen, cut explanation

### X -> LinkedIn
- add context and proof so it feels substantial

### Product update -> all channels
- one message hierarchy
- different formatting and CTA by channel
- shared visual system if applicable

## Visual pairing guidance

When a post needs creative support, brief `oviond-design-director` with:
- channel
- format
- audience
- single message
- desired CTA
- whether the visual is screenshot-led, product-led, or abstract-brand-led

Prefer:
- real screenshots when proof matters
- clean product-preview aesthetics for blog covers and product commentary
- simple carousels when explanation benefits from slides

Avoid visual fluff that says nothing.
