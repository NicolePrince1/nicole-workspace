# Editorial System

## Job to be done

Make Oviond look like the clearest, sharpest, most trustworthy answer for agencies that are tired of reporting chaos.

The content system should:
- attract the right agencies
- sharpen category perception
- support trials and onboarding confidence
- reduce the sense that Oviond is vague or unpolished
- build trust through repeated proof

## Audience priority

### Primary audience
- small to mid-sized marketing agencies
- agency owners
- heads of performance / strategy
- account leads who feel reporting pain directly

### Secondary audience
- operators evaluating reporting platforms
- existing trial users needing confidence
- agencies comparing Oviond against AgencyAnalytics, Looker Studio, Swydo, or manual reporting

## Positioning anchor

Default to this emotional outcome:
- reporting should feel **invisible, dependable, and done**

Use this to judge ideas:
- does it reduce complexity?
- does it increase trust?
- does it feel usable and credible?
- does it help an agency imagine less reporting stress?

## Editorial pillars

### 1. Reporting pain and truth
Use when exposing the real cost of messy reporting, manual reporting, broken attribution, spreadsheet hell, or client-report chaos.

### 2. Simplicity and clarity
Use when showing how Oviond reduces setup pain, keeps reporting clean, removes clutter, or makes reporting easier to maintain.

### 3. Product proof
Use screenshots, walkthroughs, feature explanations, before/after flows, integration examples, and real product behavior.

### 4. Agency operations and retention
Use when connecting reporting quality to client trust, retention, account management, margin, speed, or agency sanity.

### 5. Measurement skepticism and better practice
Use when challenging polluted metrics, weak attribution, vanity dashboards, and low-signal reporting habits.

### 6. AI and automation
Use when showing how AI should assist reporting work without replacing reliability, delivery, permissions, scheduled reporting, and client-ready trust.

## Source-mining order

Prefer topic inputs in this order:
1. real product or GTM change
2. real customer/support friction
3. real measurement lesson
4. real search demand or repeated objection
5. strong category opinion
6. lightweight educational filler only if better inputs are temporarily unavailable

## Default content mix

Target a practical mix like:
- 40% product proof and education
- 20% agency pain / category truth
- 20% measurement skepticism / insight
- 10% company or roadmap updates
- 10% experimental formats or bolder POV

## Canonical source principle

Do not start by writing five platform variants in parallel.
Start with one source asset that carries the actual thinking:
- a blog article for depth
- a LinkedIn post for a concise strong argument
- an X thread for sharper faster commentary

Then adapt outward.

## Topic scorecard

Before producing, pressure-test the idea:
- **Relevance:** does the target agency actually care?
- **Specificity:** is there a real example, proof point, or lesson?
- **Differentiation:** could any SaaS company post this, or is it ours?
- **Repurposability:** can it become multiple assets?
- **Business value:** does it support acquisition, activation, retention, or trust?

Kill weak topics early.

## CTA guidance

Pick one CTA only.
Default CTA types:
- read the full article
- start a free trial
- see the feature / integration
- rethink your reporting setup
- comment with pain point / opinion

Do not stuff multiple CTAs into one post.

## Tone rules

- direct
- commercially literate
- sharp
- useful
- lightly opinionated
- not corporate syrup
- not hypebeast startup nonsense

Sound like someone who actually understands agencies and reporting, because we do.
