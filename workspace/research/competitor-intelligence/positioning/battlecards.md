# Quick Battlecards

Last updated: 2026-05-07

Use these internally for positioning, sales responses, pricing-page logic, and launch copy. Public copy should stay positive unless Chris explicitly approves named competitor comparisons.

## Universal Oviond counter-position

> Oviond is for agencies that want client reporting to be simple to buy, easy to operate, and polished enough to send — without decoding feature gates, source credits, dashboard limits, user seats, or API add-ons.

## Against direct agency reporting tools

### AgencyAnalytics

- **They win on:** category awareness, agency-specific breadth, client portals, many integrations, AI/features, established trust.
- **Pressure point:** meaningful features sit behind higher plans: branding/custom domain/custom email above entry, API on higher tier, AI depth/forecasting higher, per-additional-client math.
- **Oviond line:** “AgencyAnalytics is powerful. Oviond is simpler to buy: choose client count and get the reporting system, including the essentials agencies expect.”

### Whatagraph

- **They win on:** polished reporting, AI/reporting narrative, unlimited users/reports, marketing intelligence positioning.
- **Pressure point:** source-credit pricing; paid entry is high and annual-oriented; API/advanced destinations/custom capabilities gated.
- **Oviond line:** “Pay by client, not by every connected ad account, property, store, or data source.”

### DashThis

- **They win on:** easy dashboards, clear agency/reporting positioning, all integrations, unlimited users.
- **Pressure point:** pricing by dashboards and sources; AI Pro add-on; dashboard-as-report model can create expansion math.
- **Oviond line:** “Dashboards should follow the client relationship, not become another unit to count.”

### Swydo

- **They win on:** simple ‘one plan, all features’ language, unlimited users, no client/seat limits.
- **Pressure point:** data-source pricing can still force account-level forecasting.
- **Oviond line:** “Swydo is close on simplicity. Oviond’s sharper promise is client-count pricing plus a broader reporting system.”

### Reporting Ninja

- **They win on:** very low pricing, “all features included” language, 3-in-1 hosted reporting + Looker Studio + Google Sheets connector value.
- **Pressure point:** narrower connector/product depth, report/user/account counters, weaker visible API/MCP/AI posture, and possible lower-polish perception.
- **Oviond line:** “Reporting Ninja is a sharp budget tool. Oviond is the more complete agency reporting system: broader, more polished, better supported, and future-ready.”

### Reportz.io

- **They win on:** lightweight KPI dashboards, low-cost simplicity, shareable/password-protected client visibility.
- **Pressure point:** current direct-site verification was blocked; pricing/product transparency and modern API/AI/MCP posture are weaker.
- **Oviond line:** “Good for simple KPI links. Oviond is for agencies that need a reliable branded reporting operation.”

### Octoboard / ReportGarden / Raven / Metrics Watch

- **They win on:** niche fit — white-label dashboards, agency reporting, SEO reporting, email-first reporting.
- **Pressure point:** narrower, older, or less comprehensive than the full agency reporting system Oviond wants to be.
- **Oviond line:** “Good for narrow jobs. Oviond is the clearer system for multi-channel agency reporting.”

## Against BI/dashboard substitutes

### Looker Studio

- **They win on:** free, flexible, familiar Google ecosystem, huge template/connector ecosystem.
- **Pressure point:** not a client-reporting system; third-party connector costs; permissions/QA/maintenance burden; no true white-label portal.
- **Oviond line:** “Looker Studio is a canvas. Oviond is the reporting operation.”

### Databox

- **They win on:** strong dashboards, agency/client account features, templates, AI/MCP/API posture.
- **Pressure point:** data-source/add-on complexity; white label and specialist support are not simply included.
- **Oviond line:** “Databox is a strong dashboard platform. Oviond should feel calmer: fewer pricing dimensions, more agency-reporting defaults.”

### Klipfolio

- **They win on:** custom dashboards, white-label add-ons, REST/API flexibility, agency/OEM use cases.
- **Pressure point:** dashboard engineering, add-ons, custom setup, complexity.
- **Oviond line:** “You should not need a dashboard engineering practice to deliver client reports.”

### Power BI / Tableau / Domo

- **They win on:** enterprise BI, modeling, governance, executive analytics credibility.
- **Pressure point:** overkill for SMB agency reporting; seat/capacity/contract/admin complexity; white label is an embedded/developer path.
- **Oviond line:** “Enterprise BI solves a different job. Oviond solves agency client reporting.”

## Against connector/report automation layers

### Supermetrics

- **They win on:** huge connector brand, marketing data into BI/Sheets/warehouses/AI, API/MCP story.
- **Pressure point:** package + destination + data source + accounts + users + refresh + API tiers; still leaves agencies building reports elsewhere.
- **Oviond line:** “Supermetrics moves data. Oviond turns reporting into a client-ready system.”

### Power My Analytics

- **They win on:** low-cost agency connector economics, Looker Studio/Sheets/Excel focus, Data Builder, broad practical sources, Pro plan value.
- **Pressure point:** still a data layer; agencies maintain external dashboards, branding, permissions, QA, delivery, and commentary.
- **Oviond line:** “Power My Analytics makes DIY reporting cheaper. Oviond makes DIY reporting unnecessary.”

### Funnel / Improvado / Adverity

- **They win on:** marketing data hub, modeling, governance, enterprise data operations.
- **Pressure point:** high cost/complexity; data-platform language; not SMB agency reporting first.
- **Oviond line:** “If reporting is a data-platform project, use a data platform. If it is an agency deliverable, use Oviond.”

### Porter / Dataslayer / Coupler / Windsor / Two Minute Reports

- **They win on:** low-cost connectors, Looker/Sheets/Power BI destinations, AI/MCP momentum.
- **Pressure point:** still DIY; agencies manage templates, branding, client permissions, QA, delivery, archive, and commentary.
- **Oviond line:** “Cheap connectors do not remove the reporting workload. Oviond packages the whole loop.”

## Against channel-specific suites

### Semrush

- **They win on:** SEO/competitive intelligence, brand, data depth, API/MCP now live.
- **Pressure point:** modular pricing/toolkits/API units; not a broad client-reporting portal for all agency channels.
- **Oviond line:** “Use Semrush for SEO intelligence. Use Oviond to deliver the client reporting experience across every channel.”

### Optmyzr

- **They win on:** deep PPC optimization, automation, budget pacing, Shopping/PMax/feed workflows, white-label paid-media reporting, practical AI.
- **Pressure point:** PPC-first and ad-spend priced; API/custom data is Enterprise/on-request; it does not solve full-client cross-channel reporting.
- **Oviond line:** “Use Optmyzr to optimize paid media. Use Oviond to report the whole client relationship.”

### SE Ranking / BrightLocal

- **They win on:** SEO/local SEO agency reporting, white label/client seats, scheduled reports, AI summaries, API/MCP in SE Ranking.
- **Pressure point:** narrow channel fit; add-ons/project/keyword/location logic.
- **Oviond line:** “Excellent for SEO-only or local-only reporting. Oviond is for agencies needing one cross-channel branded reporting layer.”

### Ahrefs / Moz

- **They win on:** SEO data, rank/backlink/site audit depth.
- **Pressure point:** not full agency client reporting systems; weak native portal/white-label posture compared with dedicated reporting platforms.
- **Oviond line:** “SEO tools are inputs. Oviond is the report your client actually receives.”

### Sprout / Hootsuite

- **They win on:** social publishing/inbox/listening/analytics workflows.
- **Pressure point:** social-only reporting; expensive seat/module structures; not cross-channel agency reporting.
- **Oviond line:** “Use social tools to run social. Use Oviond to report the whole client relationship.”

### HubSpot

- **They win on:** CRM gravity, attribution, automation, dashboards inside the client’s operating system.
- **Pressure point:** one-client/one-CRM gravity; not built for agency-wide multi-client white-label reporting across many platforms.
- **Oviond line:** “HubSpot reports HubSpot. Oviond reports the agency’s client portfolio.”

## Against AI-native workflows

### ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini + Sheets/Looker

- **They win on:** ad hoc analysis, narrative drafts, low-cost spreadsheet workflows.
- **Pressure point:** no durable client portal, permissioned multi-client reporting, branded archive, repeatable metric governance, or scheduled delivery.
- **Oviond line:** “AI can explain the numbers. Oviond keeps the numbers connected, branded, scheduled, permissioned, and client-ready.”

### Julius / Polymer / ThoughtSpot / Copilot / Tableau/Looker AI

- **They win on:** conversational analysis and dashboards.
- **Pressure point:** either too generic, too enterprise, or not agency-delivery-first.
- **Oviond line:** “Ask questions anywhere. Deliver client reporting from one trusted system.”

## Objection responses

### “We can do this in Looker Studio for free.”

You can build reports in Looker Studio. The real cost is maintaining the reporting operation: connectors, templates, permissions, branding, QA, scheduling, client access, and monthly commentary. Oviond is for agencies that want that system packaged.

### “We already use Semrush/Ahrefs/SE Ranking.”

Keep using them. Oviond is not replacing specialist SEO tools; it gives clients one clear reporting experience across SEO, ads, social, analytics, email, and more.

### “AI can write our reports now.”

AI can draft commentary. It still needs trusted data, approved metrics, client context, branded delivery, review, and an archive. Oviond should become the trusted reporting layer AI can work with.

### “AgencyAnalytics/Whatagraph/Databox already does this.”

They are good tools. Oviond’s difference is pricing and operating simplicity: choose your client count, get the core agency reporting system, and stop upgrading around essentials.
