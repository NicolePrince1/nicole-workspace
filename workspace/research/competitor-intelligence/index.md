## Market map

### 1. Direct agency reporting platforms

**Primary launch comparison set:** AgencyAnalytics, Whatagraph, DashThis, Swydo.

These are the closest alternatives when an agency is actively choosing reporting software. They mostly compete on templates, integrations, client portals, report automation, and agency-specific workflows. The pattern: they often say “simple,” but packaging still creates gates around clients, source credits, dashboards, reports, users, white label, AI, API, or support.

### 2. BI/dashboard substitutes

**Primary substitution stack:** Looker Studio + connectors, Databox, Klipfolio, Power BI/Tableau for more mature teams.

They win when agencies want flexibility, free/low-cost entry, or enterprise BI credibility. They lose when the agency needs client-ready delivery, white-label portals, recurring reporting operations, and less setup maintenance.

### 3. Connector/report automation layers

**Primary stack threat:** Supermetrics, Porter Metrics, Dataslayer, Coupler, Windsor, Funnel.

These tools do not usually replace Oviond as a client portal. They replace the data layer and push agencies toward assembling their own reporting stack in Looker Studio, Sheets, Power BI, BigQuery, or AI tools. The strategic threat is not their dashboards; it is that they are racing to own “marketing data into AI.”

### 4. Channel-specific suites

**Highest risk for narrow agencies:** SE Ranking/BrightLocal for SEO/local SEO, Semrush for SEO/competitive intelligence, HubSpot for CRM-led clients, Sprout/Hootsuite for social-only retainers.

They substitute when the agency lives inside a single channel suite and client reporting does not need a cross-channel, white-labeled reporting layer.

### 5. AI-native / MCP threats

**Highest future threat:** ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini + Sheets/Looker + connectors/MCP.

The danger is not one AI startup. The danger is workflow displacement: agencies asking an assistant to pull data, explain what changed, draft commentary, and update client-facing artifacts. Oviond should expose secure, scoped API/MCP capabilities while keeping public positioning calm and human: simple agency reporting, not AI hype.

## Core market truth

The category is crowded, but most vendors still make agencies do pricing math. They ask agencies to count clients, dashboards, source credits, data accounts, seats, destinations, API units, refresh rates, AI responses, support tiers, or add-ons.

Oviond's cleanest opening is not “we have more features.” It is:

> Choose the number of clients you report on. Get the reporting system. Stop decoding plans.

## Launch rule

Lead with clarity. Let AI/API/MCP act as proof that Oviond is future-ready, not as the headline that makes non-technical agency owners nervous.
