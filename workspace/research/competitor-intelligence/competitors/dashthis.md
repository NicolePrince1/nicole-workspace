## DashThis

Last verified: 2026-05-07
Priority: Tier 1
Category: Direct agency reporting
Website: https://dashthis.com

## Quick read

- **What they are:** Easy-to-use marketing reporting software built around dashboards/reports.
- **Best-fit customer:** Agencies and marketers who want fast report templates, automated delivery, and white-label dashboards without deep BI complexity.
- **Core promise:** Clear dashboard/source pricing and simple marketing reports.
- **Main Oviond relevance:** Very close SMB-agency alternative; strong simplicity claim but dashboard/source scaling creates a wedge.

## Positioning and messaging

- Pricing promise: “Pricing Built Around Dashboards & Sources.”
- Target audience: Agencies and marketers.
- Key language/claims: One dashboard = one marketing report; 30+/34+ integrations; unlimited users; unlimited client accounts; AI Insights; white-label dashboards.
- Emotional angle: Reporting should be quick, visual, and low-effort.

## Pricing and packaging

- **Pricing model:** Dashboards/reports plus source allowances.
- **Entry price:** Individual: **$54/mo monthly** or **$44/mo paid yearly**, includes 3 dashboards and 15 sources. Professional: $164 monthly / $139 annual for 10 dashboards and 40 sources. Business: $324 monthly / $279 annual for 25 dashboards and 100 sources. Standard begins at $499 monthly / $429 annual for 50 dashboards and 200 sources, then scales upward.
- **Scaling metric:** Dashboards and sources.
- **Free trial/demo:** Free trial CTA; white-label page says get 10 dashboards for 14 days.
- **Annual-pressure:** Annual pricing prominently discounted.
- **Key gates:**
  - Clients/accounts: Website claims unlimited client accounts.
  - Data sources/connectors: Source allowances now included; unlimited integrations/access language, but source count is part of pricing.
  - Dashboards/reports: Primary gate.
  - White label: White-label options and custom domain promoted; historically full white label may require higher dashboard volume, but current fetched pages say white-label dashboards and all plans/trials include features. Needs plan-detail verification before public comparison.
  - Custom domains: Custom domain available for white-label dashboards.
  - Users/seats: Unlimited users.
  - AI: AI Insights and Chat Mode available to everyone per feature page.
  - API/MCP: No custom API support verified; CSV and Google Sheets/custom data are emphasized instead.
  - Refresh rate: Dashboards update automatically; exact refresh intervals not verified.
  - Support/implementation: Product Specialist access mentioned.

## Product and capability map

- Dashboards/reports: One dashboard equals one marketing report; PDF, URL, scheduled email.
- Templates: 50+ report templates, 1000+ preset widgets, cloning, custom templates.
- Integrations: 30+/34+ native integrations plus CSV/Google Sheets/custom data.
- White label/client portals: Custom URL/domain, custom themes, logo, custom email, remove DashThis branding.
- Data transformation/blending: Multi-widget merging; less data-warehouse oriented than Whatagraph/TapClicks.
- Automation/scheduling: Automatic report dispatch, automated updates, PDF attachments.
- AI/narratives/insights: AI Insights, Chat Mode, dashboard context.
- API/docs/MCP: Not a visible strength.
- Onboarding/setup: Free trial-first, simple setup.

## Strengths

- Strong simplicity and SMB-agency fit.
- Unlimited users/client accounts are compelling.
- Good white-label story and easy templates.
- Lower entry price than Whatagraph and many upmarket tools.

## Weaknesses / exploitable gaps

- Dashboard/source pricing can punish agencies that want multiple views per client.
- Integration count is lower than broader platforms.
- API/data extensibility looks weaker than platforms with public API/warehouse posture.
- “Simple” story is credible, so Oviond must be even clearer: client-count pricing, not dashboard math.

## Oviond counter-position

“DashThis is simple to use. Oviond should be simpler to buy: price by clients, not by how many reports, dashboards, or connected sources each client needs.”
