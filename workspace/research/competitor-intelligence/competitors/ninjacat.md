## Strategic implications for Oviond launch messaging

1. **Lead with buyer clarity, not feature volume.** Nearly everyone claims automation, integrations, templates, and white label. The scarcer promise is “you can understand the price in 10 seconds.”
2. **Make client-count pricing the hero, but pair it with all-core-features inclusion.** AgencyAnalytics also uses clients; Swydo includes all features. Oviond’s wedge is the combination.
3. **Avoid source/report/dashboard math in public copy.** Competitors train buyers to count source credits, active data sources, dashboards, reports, campaigns/domains, AI credits, and fair usage. Oviond should say: “If you know your client count, you know your plan.”
4. **White label/custom domains should feel included, not premium.** Many agencies see white label as table stakes. Gating it creates resentment; including it makes Oviond feel agency-native.
5. **AI positioning should be practical.** Whatagraph, DashThis, Swydo, Octoboard, TapClicks, and NinjaCat all now mention AI. Oviond should avoid generic “AI insights” and frame AI/MCP/API as making reporting invisible, dependable, and operationally connected.
6. **Public posture should stay positive.** Do not dunk on named competitors. Use category language: “No source-credit math. No dashboard-count anxiety. No feature-gate scavenger hunt.”
