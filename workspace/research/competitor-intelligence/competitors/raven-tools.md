## Raven Tools

Last verified: 2026-05-07
Priority: Tier 2
Category: SEO toolset with client reporting
Website: https://raventools.com

## Quick read

- **What they are:** SEO and marketing reporting platform combining site audits, rank tracking, backlinks, competitor research, social/email/call integrations, and reports.
- **Best-fit customer:** SEO-led agencies needing reporting plus SEO tooling in one bundle.
- **Core promise:** Affordable SEO and reporting software for busy marketers.
- **Main Oviond relevance:** Competes where SEO agencies want reporting bundled with SEO tools; less direct for broad agency reporting.

## Positioning and messaging

- Pricing promise: “Affordable SEO and Reporting Software for Busy Marketers.”
- Target audience: SEO/marketing agencies and marketers.
- Key language/claims: 45 tools, automated client reports, white label, domains/campaigns, position checks, SEO/PPC/social/email/call integrations.
- Emotional angle: One roof for SEO + reporting; avoid paying for separate SEO subscriptions.

## Pricing and packaging

- **Pricing model:** Plan tiers by domains/campaigns, users, rank position checks, SEO usage.
- **Entry price:** Small Biz: **$49/mo monthly** or **$39/mo annual**, includes 2 domains/campaigns, 2 users, 1,500 position checks. Start: $109 monthly / $79 annual, 20 domains/campaigns, 4 users. Higher tiers Grow/Thrive/Lead scale to $199/$299/$479 monthly or $139/$249/$399 annual.
- **Scaling metric:** Domains/campaigns, users, rank checks, crawls/backlink usage.
- **Free trial/demo:** 7-day trial.
- **Annual-pressure:** Up to 30% annual discount/prepaid emphasis.
- **Key gates:**
  - Clients/accounts: Domains/campaigns capped by plan.
  - Data sources/connectors: 25+ sources; SEO/PPC/social/email/call tracking integrations.
  - Dashboards/reports: Automated client reports unlimited across plans.
  - White label: Subdomain + email + report on Small Biz/Start; full white label from Grow up.
  - Custom domains: Stated as available from Grow package.
  - Users/seats: Capped by plan.
  - AI: IBM Watson/Calais data integrations mentioned; no modern AI reporting story verified.
  - API/MCP: Not visible as a primary posture.
  - Refresh rate: Rank tracking daily/weekly/monthly options.
  - Support/implementation: Enterprise pricing for more resources.

## Product and capability map

- Dashboards/reports: Automated client reports, shareable URL, PDF, customizable dashboard.
- Templates: Reporting templates/tooling.
- Integrations: Moz, Majestic, GSC, GA, Bing Webmaster Tools, Google Ads, Bing Ads, Facebook Ads, social, Mailchimp/Campaign Monitor/Emma/AWeber/Constant Contact, CallRail.
- White label/client portals: Branded reports, subdomain/email, full white label on higher plans.
- Data transformation/blending: Less modern data-blending posture; more SEO/report widgets.
- Automation/scheduling: Automated client reports; rank tracking frequency control.
- AI/narratives/insights: Not a prominent modern AI threat.
- API/docs/MCP: Not verified.
- Onboarding/setup: Free trial; credit card after trial.

## Strengths

- Strong SEO tool bundle beyond reporting.
- Very low entry price.
- Established agency use cases and white-label reporting.
- Generous domains/users at annual Start/Grow relative to some competitors.

## Weaknesses / exploitable gaps

- Not a pure reporting platform; product gravity is SEO tooling.
- Multiple usage caps make packaging harder to compare.
- Full white label/custom domain are gated above entry plans.
- Modern AI/client-portal/API story is weaker than newer competitors.

## Oviond counter-position

“For SEO-led teams, Raven bundles useful tools. Oviond should not try to out-SEO Raven; it should win agencies that want cleaner cross-channel client reporting without SEO-tool packaging complexity.”
