# Oviond Competitor Intelligence

Last updated: 2026-05-07
Owner: Nicole Prince

## Purpose

This is the durable competitor-research base for Oviond's launch positioning. It is designed to be easy to access, reference, and update as pricing, feature gates, AI/API positioning, and market language change.

## Strategic lens

Oviond's current launch position to pressure-test against the market:

> Radically simple agency reporting: one clear client-count slider, every core feature included, white label/custom domains/API/MCP in the background, no confusing plan gates, built to make reporting invisible, dependable, and done.

Public posture: positive and confident, not attack-style competitor callouts.

## Research structure

- `index.md` — executive market map, top threats, and where to start.
- `HOW_TO_UPDATE.md` — operating instructions for keeping the research current.
- `competitor-universe.csv` — master list of researched competitors, priorities, profile paths, and status.
- `competitors/` — one durable profile per competitor/threat.
- `categories/` — canonical landscape research by competitor type.
- `pricing/pricing-and-gate-map.md` — pricing models, feature gates, and Oviond's pricing opportunity.
- `positioning/launch-positioning-implications.md` — launch strategy synthesis and messaging implications.
- `positioning/battlecards.md` — quick internal counter-positioning by competitor group.
- `sources/source-log.md` — extracted source URLs for re-checking.
- `raw/` — raw/source-backed category research retained for traceability.
- `archive/` — obsolete snapshots retained for context.

## Update rules

1. Prefer primary sources: pricing pages, docs, product pages, changelogs, help centers, terms, API docs.
2. Use secondary sources only to discover angles or validate customer sentiment.
3. Every competitor profile should include source URLs and a `Last verified` date.
4. Separate facts from Nicole's interpretation.
5. Track feature gates explicitly — especially white label, custom domains, users, clients/accounts, data sources, dashboards/reports, AI, API/MCP, refresh rate, support, and annual commitments.
6. Keep `competitor-universe.csv`, relevant profile files, category docs, pricing map, and positioning docs in sync when a change matters.
7. Do not store secrets or scraped customer/private data here.
8. Public launch copy should not directly dunk on named competitors unless Chris explicitly approves.

## Competitor profile standard

Each major competitor profile should answer:

- What they are
- Best-fit customer
- Core promise / positioning
- Pricing model and gates
- Feature coverage
- Integrations/data model
- White-label/client reporting posture
- AI/API/automation posture
- Onboarding and ease-of-use signals
- Strengths
- Weaknesses / exploitable gaps
- Likely objection they create against Oviond
- Oviond counter-position
- Sources
