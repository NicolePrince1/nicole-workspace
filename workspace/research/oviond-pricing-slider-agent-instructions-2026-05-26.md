# Oviond Pricing Slider Agent Instructions

## Context

We are finalising the new Oviond pricing page, specifically the dynamic client-count pricing slider.

The page needs to match the new app launch positioning:

- One simple agency reporting plan
- Every core feature included
- Pricing mainly scales by client count
- No confusing Starter / Pro / Advanced tiers
- No hidden feature gates
- No upgrade traps
- API and MCP included, but not used as the main emotional headline
- Lead with clarity, white-label reporting, automation, and agency scale

Important product correction: **remove “client portals” everywhere. Client portal is not a feature.**

---

## Approved Pricing Model

Use this pricing model throughout the slider and supporting copy:

- Minimum slider value: **5 clients**
- Entry price: **$49/month**
- Entry per-client equivalent: **$9.80 per client/month**
- Maximum slider value: **1,000 clients**
- Top price: **$4,900/month**
- Over 1,000 clients: talk to sales / custom volume plan

The $49 entry point is deliberate. It gives Oviond a more aggressive, competitor-aligned starting point while keeping the page simple and scalable.

Do not mention legacy pricing or customer grandfathering on this slider unless there is a separate migration/customer notice section.

---

## Core Messaging Direction

The pricing slider should communicate one idea fast:

> Pick your client count. Get the full platform. No tier games.

The message should feel simple, confident, and commercially sharp.

Oviond is not trying to sound like another bloated SaaS pricing page. The page should make agencies feel like:

- “I understand this immediately.”
- “I know what I’m paying for.”
- “I’m not going to get trapped by feature gates.”
- “This can scale with my agency.”
- “API/MCP is there when I need it, but I don’t need to be technical to buy this.”

## Add Client Definition Near Slider

Add a small helper line near the slider or pricing card:

> A client means one reporting profile, brand, store, franchise branch, or business you report on.

This is important because agencies may manage franchises, multi-location brands, stores, or multiple business profiles. The pricing page needs to make the unit of billing clear without adding friction.

---

## What To Simplify

Simplify the pricing page by removing repeated claims.

Do not say “every feature included” in every block. Use the idea strategically, then let the structure prove it.

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Hero: one simple plan for agency reporting
2. Slider: choose client count, price updates
3. Card: $49/month for 5 clients
4. Included section: no Pro tier, no Advanced tier, no upgrade traps
5. Feature grid: short descriptions for what is included
6. CTA: start trial

---
