- Campaign-level LPV read:
  - `Remarketing USA - Free Trial Test` spent `R624.59`, generated `52` link clicks and `29` landing page views, for `R21.54` cost per landing page view.

## Important quality caveat

- The test’s apparent win is heavily driven by `Audience Network`, not by a broad cross-platform improvement.
- Placement breakdown for `Remarketing USA - Free Trial Test`:
  - `Audience Network`: `R76.69` spend, `26` LPVs, `R2.95` LPV CPA
  - `Facebook`: `R337.48` spend, `3` LPVs, `R112.49` LPV CPA
  - `Instagram`: `R208.20` spend, `0` LPVs
- By contrast, the control’s Facebook placement was stronger than the test’s Facebook placement on LPV CPA (`R79.80` vs `R112.49`), though the control still lost badly overall because the test found much cheaper traffic elsewhere.
- Practical implication: the test is winning on cheap landing-page traffic, but the quality of that traffic is not proven yet and may be inflated by Audience Network.

## Conversion / signal caution

- Campaign reporting still showed `0` leads and `0` purchases` across the current read window.
- A custom conversion action key (`offsite_conversion.custom.936973485167575`) is firing in reporting, but its volume pattern does not yet look reliable enough to treat as the deciding business KPI without mapping it cleanly.
- Do not scale budgets yet based on LPV efficiency alone.

## Recommended next move

- Keep the control and test running for now.
- Hold budget scaling until we verify downstream quality beyond cheap LPVs.
- Next best step is a tighter quality check on the test traffic, especially whether Audience Network traffic leads to real trial starts / signups rather than just cheap page loads.
