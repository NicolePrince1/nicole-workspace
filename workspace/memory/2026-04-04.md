- Important strategic interpretation: Oviond likely has a durable-value / stickiness problem, not just a pricing problem.

## Deep GA4 audit: what the property is really telling us

- GA4 property `Oviond Website + App` is not a clean marketing-site property. It combines:
  - `www.oviond.com` marketing traffic
  - `v2.oviond.com` product/app usage
  - many white-label customer report domains
  - some localhost/dev traffic
- This means top-level GA4 channel, country, landing-page, and conversion reports are structurally contaminated if read as simple acquisition truth.
- The GA4 `conversions` metric is currently not trustworthy as a finance-grade or growth-grade KPI:
  - last 30 days showed `150,917` conversions vs `21,244` in the prior 30 days while sessions only moved from `35,082` to `44,128`
  - daily conversions jumped sharply in early March without a matching traffic jump
  - this strongly suggests non-business key events were marked as conversions / key events
- The most usable signup-style event in GA4 right now appears to be `ads_conversion_signup`, but even that should be cross-checked against app/backend truth and ad-platform reality.
- Strong campaign anomaly found:
  - GA4 shows campaign `Display Remarketing USA Feb 2026` driving the majority of website sessions and a large share of signup-style events
  - but the traffic is overwhelmingly South Africa, not USA
  - on `www.oviond.com`, that campaign showed about `10,515` South Africa sessions vs only `95` United States sessions over the last 30 days
  - on `v2.oviond.com`, `ads_conversion_signup` for that same campaign showed `219` South Africa events vs `50` United States
  - this reinforces the current decision to treat that Display campaign as suspect / waste-risk rather than as a trusted growth engine
- By contrast, the smaller PMAX and Meta signup-event reads looked much more US-aligned than the Display remarketing campaign.
- Marketing-site reality after host segmentation:
  - `www.oviond.com` traffic surged vs the prior 30 days, but was dominated by Display and homepage traffic
  - Display was about two-thirds of website sessions
  - homepage `/` was the dominant landing page by far
  - South Africa dominated website traffic, which is strategically misaligned with the current USA growth focus
- SEO / content reality on `www.oviond.com`:
  - homepage still dominates organic landings
  - strongest non-homepage organic pages in this pull were around dashboard templates, free AI tools, onboarding checklist, and reporting/software topics
  - useful signal exists, but the SEO layer does not yet look like a tightly buyer-intent-led comparison funnel
- Attribution / measurement interpretation:
  - GA4 can still help with pattern-spotting and segmented behavior analysis
  - but it should not be the single source of truth for trial quality, paid-channel scaling, or business performance until conversion definitions are cleaned up and acquisition views are separated properly
- Chris explicitly asked that these GA4 findings be remembered and carried forward into strategy, channel evaluation, and future decision-making rather than treated as a one-off audit.

## US-only TAM + AI / LLM risk framing

- Strategic read after the 2026-04-04 market-size / risk discussion:
  - the US agency market appears large enough for Oviond’s current scale ambition, including the $100k MRR goal
  - the immediate constraint is not TAM; it is execution, retention, measurement, and finding a repeatable US acquisition engine
- Practical interpretation:
  - even a relatively narrow US-only serviceable slice of agencies should be sufficient for the current goal if Oviond can improve customer quality and stickiness
  - reaching $100k does not require mass-market dominance; it requires a small but meaningful penetration of a focused US agency segment
- AI / LLM threat read:
  - real risk exists at the low end of the category
  - LLMs can increasingly replace manual report writing, narrative summaries, and one-off analysis for smaller or more technical agencies
  - the weaker / more commoditized part of the category is static dashboarding and generic monthly reporting output
- More defensible value layer for Oviond:
  - reliable integrations and data refresh
  - cross-source normalization
  - white-label and client-ready delivery
  - permissions / sharing / repeatability
  - scheduled reporting workflows
  - trust and low-admin usability for agencies
- Product / positioning implication:
  - Oviond should not think of itself as only a dashboard vendor
  - it should move toward an AI-assisted agency reporting operating system with trusted delivery and operational simplicity
