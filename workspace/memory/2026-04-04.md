# 2026-04-04

## Churn / retention became a core strategic priority this week

- Chris asked that the churn work be remembered properly and treated as an important part of Oviond’s strategy for the coming week and for hitting growth goals.
- Stripe-led churn audit so far says the main problem is not failed payments; it is mostly voluntary churn.
- Working retention thesis:
  - churn is concentrated in monthly plans more than annual plans
  - biggest reason buckets are `unused`, `switched_service`, and complexity / trust issues
  - price appears more like a symptom of weak perceived value than the core driver in most cases
  - the problem is not only onboarding; many churners stayed for months or years before leaving
- Product / experience warnings surfaced in cancellation comments:
  - reports not ready to send to clients
  - too difficult / irritating to use
  - resetting and re-integrating pain
  - one customer said the platform got worse over time
- Current highest-priority workstreams for the week:
  1. delinquency rescue / saveable revenue recovery
  2. recent high-value churn win-backs
  3. mature monthly-to-annual conversion
  4. deeper product retention work around activation, client-ready report confidence, and integration stability
- Important cohort pattern: Small Agency Monthly and some legacy monthly plan families appear especially fragile.
- Important strategic interpretation: Oviond likely has a durable-value / stickiness problem, not just a pricing problem.
