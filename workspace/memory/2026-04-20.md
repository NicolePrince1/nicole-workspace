## Pricing simplification idea was pressure-tested against live Stripe subscriptions

- Chris proposed a much simpler pricing model: one public slider, all core features included, likely starting around `49` or `59`, stepping by client bands, and capping self-serve around `100` clients at roughly `749` to `800`.
- Nicole checked the live Stripe subscription mix to understand the real impact of removing the old ultra-low entry point.
- Main finding: there were effectively no meaningful live core subscriptions sitting exactly on a public `$15` monthly plan.
- The real low-end active core/legacy cohort was small:
  - `15` active core/legacy subscriptions below `$49`
  - current combined monthly value about `$480.58`
- Broader active paid subscriptions below `$49` totaled `22` accounts and about `$556.40` monthly, but this included small non-core items such as add-ons.
- Modeled upside if all active paid subscriptions below the threshold moved cleanly:
  - floor at `$49` -> about `+$521.60` monthly across active subscriptions
  - floor at `$59` -> about `+$802.60` monthly across active subscriptions
- Important interpretation: the extra theoretical gain from pushing the floor from `$49` to `$59` was only about `$281` monthly at the active-paid level, and only about `$201` monthly if focusing on the small active core/legacy cohort. That is not enough to justify unnecessary churn or friction by itself.
- The more meaningful migration wrinkle was not a `$15` base but a small legacy sub-`49` cohort around prices like `39`, `29`, `24`, `19`, and `14`, plus a few AppSumo/custom oddities that should not shape the new public pricing page.
- Nicole’s recommendation to Chris:
  - yes, simplify to one slider
  - yes, include all core features
  - yes, remove the old cheap public entry points
  - yes, cap self-serve around `100` clients and use custom pricing above that
  - prefer a `$49` starting point over `$59`
- Reasoning: `$49` appears closer to already-proven live pricing, gives most of the simplicity benefit, and avoids overreaching for a relatively small additional revenue upside.
