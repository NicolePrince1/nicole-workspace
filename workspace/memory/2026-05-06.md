# 2026-05-06

## Oviond AI-first positioning and pricing direction from Chris

- Chris said the coming massive app update plus new website should reposition Oviond strategically as AI-first, but not as a weak in-product chatbot.
- Strategic direction: strip half-baked AI features from the product UI; keep the core product stupid simple, highly reliable, and easy to use, while making Oviond a first-class API/MCP platform with docs already at `docs.oviond.com`.
- Chris's thesis: the new way of working will happen inside the tools people already use — ChatGPT, Codex, Claude Desktop, and whatever comes next — so Oviond should meet agencies there through a powerful MCP server/API while still bridging current dashboard/reporting workflows for agencies that are not agent-native yet.
- Pricing direction: collapse complicated tiered/feature-gated plans into one clear plan where every customer gets the full product and mainly pays by client volume.
- Included-for-all direction: white label, custom domains, API access, MCP/AI access, templates, integrations, dashboards, reports, automation, client portals, reporting tools, unlimited team members, and no "upgrade to unlock this feature" friction.
- Nicole's strategic read: lead with "simple agency reporting with a powerful API/MCP for the AI-first era" rather than generic AI assistant language; guard against overpromising unlimited API/MCP usage, custom-domain complexity, and confusing "AI Assistant included" messaging if in-app AI is being removed.
- Chris added concrete pricing direction: one public client-count slider from 5 clients at `$59/mo` to 1,000 clients at `$4,900/mo`, with per-client cost dropping from about `$11.75/client` to `$4.90/client` at scale.
- The 1-client tier is being eliminated because it attracts time-wasters, high-support, low-LTV customers. The old 100-client ceiling is being lifted to 1,000 because real Oviond customers already operate at 400, 500, and 900 clients, and the strategy is to make the savings story visible and shareable.
- Chris's competitive-pricing read: the market makes money through gates and hidden expansion costs. AgencyAnalytics gates white-label, AI, and multi-account access; Databox gates white-label as a paid add-on and killed its free plan; Whatagraph pushes annual lock-in and source credits; Klipfolio gates refresh rate/dashboards/SSO; DashThis recently abandoned per-dashboard pricing because it did not reflect real usage. Oviond's counter-position should be transparent pricing that does not punish growth.
- Chris's MCP-first read: as of April 2026, major competitors have not shipped MCP support, creating a 6–12 month first-mover window to own "run your client reporting from any AI tool you already use." Entry-tier API/MCP access is the wedge.
- Core bets Chris named: (1) agencies are ready to reward credible no-gate pricing, (2) MCP-first positioning is defensible long enough to build category voice, and (3) existing 100+ client customers will absorb modest increases in exchange for simplicity, API/MCP access, and transparent public pricing.
- Chris named launch risks/requirements: grandfathering or migration credits for existing customers because the 100-client price moves from `$449` to `$700` and entry moves from `$39/3 clients` to `$59/5 clients`; a clear definition of "client" to handle franchise/multi-region/multi-account setups; two-track messaging because API/MCP excites technical buyers but intimidates most buyers; custom client domains must be bounded self-service capped at `1 + client count` with rate limits, orphan cleanup, and cert monitoring; pricing-page comparison calculators should make competitor savings interactive; and a 90-day switcher concierge should capture agencies leaving competitors after price/surcharge/free-plan changes.
- One-line strategy from Chris: Oviond is becoming the simplest, most transparent reporting platform in the market — one plan, every feature, AI-native, priced honestly per client — using the MCP window to plant the flag before the market wakes up.
